History

In mid-2022, we discovered unauthorized withdrawals of 50 BTC from our Binance account. We immediately tried to resolve the issue through Binance’s official support channels. Unfortunately, Binance’s response was slow and unsatisfactory, leading to frustration. Over the following months, we submitted numerous support tickets and participated in phone calls, but received little helpful guidance.

Having exhausted all direct avenues, we realized that public pressure might be needed. We decided to document our experience online to raise awareness and seek help from the community. Through social media and online forums, we shared our story to gather support and attention. The more our story spread, the more pressure Binance would feel to address this issue.

Plan

We have formulated a multi-pronged plan to seek the return of the funds and prevent such injustices from happening to others. Our campaign combines public exposure, legal action, and community engagement in several stages.

  1. Public Awareness Campaign – Share our story through press releases, social media, and community forums.
  2. Support Petition – Create an online petition gathering support from the cryptocurrency community and victims of similar cases.
  3. Legal Coordination – Coordinate with consumer protection lawyers and experts to explore legal options and prepare for potential legal actions.
  4. Industry Advocacy – Engage cryptocurrency watchdogs and legislators to highlight exchange accountability and push for stronger regulations.
  5. Victim Support Network – Organize a support forum (e.g., Discord/Telegram) for victims to share experiences and resources.
  6. Regulatory & Enforcement – File complaints with financial regulators (e.g., FinCEN, SEC) and law enforcement agencies about Binance’s violations.
  7. Public Pressure Campaign – Organize letter-writing campaigns and call-ins to Binance’s leadership and regulators to demand action, and encourage media outlets to cover the story.
